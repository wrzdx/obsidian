There exists $c_{1}$ and $c_2$ for any $n \geq n_{0}$ such that $c_{1} \cdot (n^{b}) \geq(n+a)^{b} \geq c_{2} \cdot (n^{b})$

$$c_{1} \geq (\frac{n+a}{n})^{b} = (1 + \frac{a}{n})^{b} \leq (1+a)^{b}$$
So, $c_{1} \geq (1+a)^{b}$ and $c_{2} \leq (1+a)^{b}$ when $a \geq0$ and $b \geq 0$

$$c_{1} \geq (\frac{n+a}{n})^{b} = (1 + \frac{a}{n})^{b}$$
